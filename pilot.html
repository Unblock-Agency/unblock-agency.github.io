PILOT PROGRAM MILESTONES

Month 1: Initial Assessment and Sprint Setup

Milestone: Complete baseline assessment, establish goals and sub-goals, and initiate the first two sprint cycles.

Activities:

  • Initial Assessment: Conduct discovery sessions with stakeholders and team members to assess the current processes, challenges, and strengths.
  • Capacity Planning: Facilitate a capacity planning session to assess team workload and resource allocation. Introduce a custom capacity tracking template.
  • Sprint 0 Setup: Conduct sprint planning for Sprint 0 to establish sprint goals, define roles, and set clear expectations.
  • Sprint 1 Setup: Begin Sprint 1 with a planning session at the start, a stakeholder review mid-sprint, and a team retrospective at the end. Focus on adjusting as needed.

Outcome: Clear understanding of team dynamics, initial capacity allocation, and alignment on goals, with the team starting their first sprint cycle.

Month 2: Roles, Responsibilities, and Sprint Optimization

Milestone: Finalize roles and responsibilities, refine team cadence, and continue optimizing sprints.

Activities:

  • Sprint 2 Setup: Plan and start Sprint 2, refining the backlog and sprint goals based on the lessons learned from Sprint 1.
  • Role Clarification: Facilitate a dedicated session to define clear roles and responsibilities, using a team norms sheet and creating a detailed report on role allocation.
  • Team Cadence: Establish a sprint calendar and ensure regular team support syncs (both synchronous and asynchronous) to keep the team aligned and on track.
  • Sprint 3 Setup: Start Sprint 3, conduct a mid-sprint review, and facilitate a team retrospective to evaluate the sprint’s success and health.

Outcome: The team has clarity on roles, regular cadence is established, and ongoing feedback ensures continuous improvement of the sprint process.

Month 3: Conflict Resolution and Continuous Feedback Integration

Milestone: Strengthen conflict resolution mechanisms and integrate continuous feedback.

Activities:

  • Sprint 4 Setup: Start Sprint 4 with a planning session to continue improving team workflows.
  • Conflict Resolution: Introduce conflict resolution systems and provide off-line support for developers, creating safe channels for resolving issues as they arise.
  • Stakeholder Review: Continue the practice of mid-sprint reviews with stakeholders to ensure alignment.
  • Sprint 5 Setup: Begin Sprint 5 with a planning session, conduct mid-sprint check-ins, and run a team retrospective at the end.

Outcome: Improved communication, fewer conflicts, and more effective feedback loops that increase team cohesion and productivity.

Month 4: Strategic Planning and Leadership Alignment

Milestone: Facilitate future planning, ensure leadership alignment, and further optimize sprints.

Activities:

  • Sprint 6 Setup: Start Sprint 6 with planning that incorporates adjustments from previous sprints.
  • Product Increment (PI) Planning: Facilitate a Product Increment (PI) planning session to help the team prepare for future milestones. This includes leadership training and a PI planning template.
  • Mid-Sprint Review: Continue stakeholder reviews and mid-sprint assessments to keep the project on track.
  • Sprint 7 Setup: Begin Sprint 7, continuing to refine team processes, conduct a mid-sprint review, and run a retrospective at the end of the sprint.

Outcome: Teams and leaders are aligned on long-term goals, with tools and techniques in place for scaling their processes in the future.

Month 5: User Interaction, Ideation, and Product Feedback

Milestone: Enhance team interactions with end-users and focus on ideation and product feedback.

Activities:

  • Sprint 8 Setup: Start Sprint 8 with planning focused on product feedback and ideation.
  • User Interaction: Schedule sessions to involve end-users and facilitate ideation workshops with developers, ensuring the team is building with user feedback in mind.
  • Mid-Sprint Review: Gather user insights, adjust product features accordingly, and continue engaging stakeholders for feedback.
  • Sprint 9 Setup: Begin Sprint 9, continue refining product decisions, and hold a final retrospective of the month.

Outcome: A deeper connection to end-users, better user-centered design decisions, and a team that is empowered to innovate based on user needs.

Month 6: Celebration, Well-Being, and Final Review

Milestone: Celebrate achievements, conduct a final health check, and review program outcomes.

Activities:

  • Sprint 10 Setup: Begin the final sprint, continuing the refined process with regular check-ins and mid-sprint reviews.
  • Celebration and Well-Being: Host a celebration (party, after-work event) to recognize achievements and team progress. Review workload management, time-off needs, and overall well-being.
  • Final Assessment: Conduct a comprehensive review of the team’s progress over the past six months. Include stakeholder feedback and team health data to assess the success of the pilot program.
  • Sprint 11 Setup: Complete Sprint 11 with a final retrospective and deliver a detailed report with recommendations for the future.

Outcome: A thriving, high-performing team with a strong culture of well-being, celebrating success, and equipped with a strategic roadmap for long-term growth.
